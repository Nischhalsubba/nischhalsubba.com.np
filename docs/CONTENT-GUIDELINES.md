# Portfolio Content Guidelines

These guidelines keep the portfolio honest, focused, and SEO-friendly.

## Voice

- Clear, confident, and human.
- Specific over broad.
- Professional, but not corporate filler.
- Use simple explanations for complex product work.

## Project Pages

Each project page should answer:

1. What is the project?
2. Who was it for?
3. What was my role?
4. What did I actually design, develop, contribute, or explore?
5. What problem did the design solve?
6. What constraints shaped the work?
7. What artifacts are available: prototype, Figma file, live site, repo, or case study?

Avoid:

- fake metrics
- exaggerated business claims
- claiming sole ownership for team work
- generic design process copy that could fit any project
- NDA-sensitive screenshots or details

## Blog Pages

Each blog page should be about one clear topic.

Good blog topics:

- Web3 UX and wallet clarity
- product design handoff
- portfolio strategy
- service website UX
- gaming interface clarity
- design systems and front-end awareness

Avoid:

- broad unrelated career advice inside technical articles
- generic AI-sounding paragraphs
- sections that do not support the article title
- keyword stuffing

## Homepage

The homepage should quickly communicate:

- who Nischhal is
- what design work he does
- what proof exists
- where to view projects
- where to contact him

Achievement numbers should only be used when verified.

## SEO Principles

- Use natural keywords in headings and body text.
- Link blog articles to relevant project pages.
- Link project pages to relevant writing when useful.
- Keep titles clear and search-friendly.
- Keep meta descriptions specific.
- Use descriptive image alt text.
